Treatment Overview

Knee replacement is a surgery to remove and replace the damaged cartilage and bone with an artificial joint. The procedure is designed to relieve pain, restore function, and improve the quality of life for individuals with severe knee arthritis or injury.

Eligibility For Treatment

To be eligible for knee replacement surgery, patients typically must meet certain criteria:

  • Severe knee pain or stiffness
  • Reduced mobility affecting daily activities
  • Failure to improve with other treatments
  • Overall health condition supporting surgery

Preparation Before Treatment

Before the surgery, your doctor will perform a thorough examination and may recommend certain tests to ensure you are a good candidate for knee replacement.

About Treatment

The surgery involves the removal of damaged cartilage and bone and replacing it with a prosthetic joint. It requires careful planning and execution by a skilled surgeon to ensure the best outcome for the patient.

Post-Treatment Care

Post-treatment care is essential for a successful recovery. Patients will typically need physical therapy and follow-up appointments to monitor their progress and ensure proper healing.

Treatment Recovery Tips

Recovery from knee replacement surgery can take several weeks to months. It is important to follow your doctor's instructions, attend physical therapy sessions, and avoid activities that could damage the new joint.
